Academic Background & Work Experience

Bachelor
During my Bachelor's degree, I immersed myself in the work of public libraries as influental institutional players within a community. I was mainly interested in how they can provide a space for public discourse, empower marginalised communities and thereby tackle problems such as the Digital Divide. As part of my studies, I delved into a case study on one particular public library's handling of right-wing literature, analysing it from a neo-institutionalist perspective considering the various challenges it faced by the different stakeholders within the community. Additionally, I interned at the district library of Pankow's Office for Diversity-Oriented Opening and topped it off with a Bachelor's thesis about assessing the availability of LGBTIQ*-literature in the district libraries of Berlin.

Master
During my Master's degree, my research interests have evolved to focus on Video Social Recommender Systems (VSRS) such as TikTok and Instagram Reels, as well as information literacy. I was particularly interested in understanding why users engage with these platforms, the contexts in which they do so, their integration into daily life, and how individuals encounter information through them. In my future research, I aim to expand this inquiry to explore the relevance criteria users apply when consuming content, the reasons behind their trust in certain information over others, and the channels they utilize when uncertain about the veracity of the information they encounter. Specifically, I am drawn to political information in contested regions, such as Taiwan and Europe, the latter of which has become increasingly contentious following the onset of the Russian invasion of Ukraine. I believe it is crucial to understand how users establish authority regarding whom to trust on VSRS and how they navigate uncertainty in order to effectively combat misinformation campaigns on these platforms. In addition to this micro-level approach, I am also interested in expanding my research to how public institutions across different countries conceptualize information and digital literacy, as well as their strategies for planning and implementing information literacy campaigns that enhance citizens' resilience to potential online dangers, particularly on VSRS. Methodologically, my recent work has centered on developing a systematic approach to studying the use of VSRS in everyday life, which presents unique challenges due to their highly personalized nature.

Among them, the one that left the biggest mark up till now, would be my work as a student assistant for the distant learning programme of the Institute for Library- and Information Science at the Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin. Not only did I have the chance to collaborate with incredible colleagues, but I also had the opportunity to take on various responsibilities such as organising lectures and student groups, and swiftly digitizing the entire program when the Covid-19 pandemic hit. These experiences were truly invaluable and continue to shape my professional growth to this day.
