Studies & Work

Academia - Bachelor
During my Bachelor's I immersed myself in the work of public libraries as active political players in a community and how they can provide a space for public discourse and empower marginalized communities. Among other things, I did a case study of one public library's handling of right-wing literature from a neo-institutionalist perspective in dealing with problems of different stakeholders within the community, interned at the District library of Pankow's Office for diversity-oriented opening and wrote my Bachelor thesis on the availability of LGBTIQ*-literature in the district libraries of Berlin.

Academia - Master
Since starting my Master in Information Science at the Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin, I have developed a passion for working at the intersection of technology, society, and academia. My two main focuses are on understanding how we interact with information systems, how these systems can either dismantle or reinforce societal structures, and how we can actively engage in digital citizenship and how Open Science and Open Data (be that Open Government Data or Open Source) can contribute to a more democratic society and academic system.
Currently, I am dedicating my efforts to studying the concept of 'Digital Resilience' as a crucial factor in modern societies.

Work
I have worked in jobs ranging from the fun-sounding, but for sugar-intake detrimental, work at a chocolate factory, to working in Covid-vaccination centers, to my current job at the Editorial Office of the dms - der moderne Staat journal.
Probably the two most noteworthy ones would be my work as a student assistant for the distant learning programme of the Institute for Library- and Information Science at the Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin and at the Library and Information Services of the Hertie School of Governance in Berlin. I was lucky enough to work with great colleagues and gain invaluable experiences at both.
